Ship Propeller Effects on Sediment Transport¶

The effects of ship propeller-induced jet flow on sediment transport were presented by Colangeli et al. (2023).

Theory¶

Efflux velocity

The magnitude of the efflux velocity generated by a propeller was estimated using the emperical formulas given by Hamill and Kee (2016) and Hamill and McGarvey (1997) (NEED UPDATE)

(104)¶\[V_0 = E_0 n D_p \sqrt{C_t}\]

where \(E_0 = (D_p/D_h)^{-0.403} C_t^{-1.79} \mbox{BAR}^{0.744}\), in which \(D_p\) and \(D_h\) are the propeller diameter and hub diameter, respectively. \(C_t\) is the C thrust parameter, and BAR is defined as the thrust and Blade Area. \(n\) is the RPM.

Axial flow distribution (NEED UPDATE)

(105)¶\[U_p = V_0 \exp \left( -2 \frac{y^{\prime 2} + h_p^2}{D_p^2} \right) \mbox{ in ZFE: } x^\prime \le \frac{D_p}{2c}\]
(106)¶\[U_p = V_0 \frac{1}{2c} \exp \left( -\frac{1}{2c^2} \frac{y^{\prime 2} + h_p^2}{x^{\prime 2}} \right) \mbox{ outside ZFE: } x^\prime \gt \frac{D_p}{2c}\]

where (\(x^\prime,y^\prime\)) represents the local coordinates with an origin at the propeller location. \(h_p\) is the height from the axial to the bed. \(c\) is the ducted-parameter. NOTE: the axial flow is only used to calculate effects on sediment transport. There is no interaction between the axial flow and ship-wakes.

Propeller effects on bottom shear stress

The propeller effects on the bottom shear stress were incorporated into the calculations of the friction velocity \(u_{*}\) and shear stress \(\tau\).

(107)¶\[u_* = \frac{\kappa}{-1+\ln (30H/k_s)} + U_p * \sqrt{C_f/2}\]
(108)¶\[\tau = \rho \left(\frac{\kappa}{-1+\ln (30H/k_s)}\right)^2 + \frac{1}{2}\rho C_f U_p^2\]

where \(C_f = 0.01 (C_h/D_p)\).

Model configuration¶

To incorporate the propeller effects into the sediment transport calculation, you need to compile the program with a flag -DPROPELLER in Makefile. For example

FLAG10 = -DVESSEL
FLAG11 = -DPROPELLER

In input.txt, specify

VESSEL_FOLDER = vessel/
NumVessel = 1

In /vessel/vessel_00001

Title: Vessel # 1
PRESSURE, 1
Length(m), Width(m), Alpha1(m),  Alpha2(m), Beta(m), P(unit)
20.0,  10.0, 0.5, 0.5, 0.5, 3.0
N_revolution,D_prop, C_thrust, D_hub, BAR, H_prop(below deck), C_duct
1.6, 3.7, 0.48, 1.1, 0.55, 2.5, 0.19
Time, X(m), Y(m)  (relative to the orgin of the coordinates)
0.0     100.0   30.0
100.0   1100.0  210.0

Note that the 5th and 6th lines are newly added lines for propeller input. In particular, the 5th line is comment line for parameter definitions, and the 6th line specifies values (floating) of those parameters.
